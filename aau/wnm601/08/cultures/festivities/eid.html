Eid

Eid is the festivals of Muslims whose celebrations begin from ‘Chaand Raat’ that is “Night of the Moon”. For Eid celebration in India, Muslims visit shopping malls and bazaars on this night to do shopping of Eid. Beautiful henna designs are drawn on the palms of women mostly young girls and they also buy bangles of various colors to go by with their dresses.

People greet each other saying ‘Eid Mubarak’ and embracing each other. People also exchange gifts. They wear new clothes and also children are given Eidi (sum of money) by the older people, in return, salaam is offered by children. The day of Eid begins with offering of Eid prayers in the Mosque by the men. Then many men tread toward graveyard to offer respect to their family members who are no more with them. Once they come back to home from mosque they go visit friends, relatives, neighbors and Murabbis who are the eldest members of the families. They join them to eat special dishes that are prepared on the occasion of eid for example, snacks, sweets and other special meal.

Some of the popular Masjids of India where huge crowd gather every year to offer prayers of Eid include; Aishbagh Idgah in Lucknow, Mecca Masjid in Hyderabad, in Kolkata prayers are offered on red road and the Jama Masjid in New Delhi. Thousands of Muslims celebrates this festival with great excitements and happiness. During the Eid Celebration in India, non-Muslim friends come to greet their friends on this day.
