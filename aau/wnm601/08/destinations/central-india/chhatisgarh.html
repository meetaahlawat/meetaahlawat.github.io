Chhatisgarh

Chhattisgarh is the 10th largest state in India. 41 percent of the area in this state is covered in forests. The state has many ancient monuments, rare wildlife, exquisitely carved temples, Buddhist sites, palaces, water falls, caves, rock paintings and hill plateaus. Most of these sites are untouched and unexplored and offer a unique and alternate experience to tourists, compared to traditional destinations which have become overcrowded. Often referred to as the small Niagara falls of India, the chitrakoot falls height is about 29 meters (95 ft.) and is the widest fall in India. The weather in the area is generally pleasant except in the summer season when it is hot due to absence of vegetation in the area.

The tribes of Chhattisgarh are another unique factor who mainly inhabit the dense forests of the state. In fact, more than 70% of the population is composed of tribals. The lifestyle of the tribal people is unique and imbibed with traditional rituals and superstitions. They are a friendly and jovial lot who are industrious and diligent. Although shrouded in poverty, they live life to the hilt and love to celebrate every joyous occasion. Food, drink, music, dance, mirth and merriment add color to their otherwise simple lives.

Chhattisgarh also has many historical towns and townships. One of them is called Sirpur and it dates back to 5th to the 8th centuries A.D. The 7th century Laxman Temple (dedicated to lord Vishnu) is considered as one of the finest brick temples of India with a stone doorframe. First discovered in 1872 by Lord Cunningham, [3] the temple is famous for its interesting carvings.
