The Coastal

The coastal cuisines of India serve rice and fish as a staple food and without the latter, the dishes are considered incomplete. We can also see Portuguese influence in goan food habits to a certain extent. The Kerela cuisine reflects the cultural presence as the Hindus are mostly vegetarian and use of coconuts is prevalent, whereas the Syrian Christians are fonder of fish and meat. The coastal cuisines use potatoes, tomatoes, pineapples, guavas, cashews, coconut oil, mustard seeds, and crispy curry leaves in their food.

Some of the coastal dishes are Goan Fish Curry, prawn curry, Fish Recheado, Bebinca, Pork Vindaloo, Rava Fried Fish, Chicken Xacuti, Chicken Cafreal, Pork Sorpotel, Goan Nevri Chemmeen Pollichathu,Kallummekkaya fry Fish Mappas, Meen Murringakka Curry, Cheera Thoran, Kozhikode Biryani, Kerala Fish Curry, Pomfret Moilee, Chicken Stew with Appams, Erissery, Karimeen Pollichathu and so on.
