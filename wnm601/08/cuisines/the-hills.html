The Hills

The food habits of people living in the hills prefer meat over vegetables and a lot of cuisines involve boiled and smoked style and also the use of bamboo shoot and dried and fermented beans are prevalent. A culture of maintaining a kitchen garden to grow their most favored herbs is in practice. The north-eastern stretch of India is well renowned for the use of fermented food while cooking. Other than the use of fermented beans and bamboo shoot, they prepare rice beer widely known as Apang, which is a must try.

The use of lettuce is widely popular and also the food is prepared using fewer spices, making it much healthier. Rice is a staple food among the hilly people and is prepared in various different ways. In Himanchal Pradesh, besides all the international cuisines served, to keep up with the tourists, the traditional food is worth a try. As much as they love vegetarian food, mon-vegetarian dishes are widely preferred. Generally, the cuisines are dominated by red meat and wheat bread.
